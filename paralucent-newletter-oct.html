6 Qs With . . . Sady Magendran, Partner. Chief Creative Officer
1. Tell us about Paralucent's creative/marketing side.
A culture of love and curiosity. This is our operating philosophy and our ethos. Curiosity underpins how we think and approach tasks, helping us find unique and creative ways to solve business problems by asking unexpected questions. We have a diverse and multidisciplinary team of some of the best creatives and leaders who have global ideas and bring stories vividly to life through branding, digital, video, social, scripts, and graphic design. We have a collection of unicorns.
2. What's the key to making clients happy?
Our curiosity, diversity of voices, worldly experiences—and range of work we have been involved with—naturally brings fresh and relevant ideas. It’s about opening their eyes to something different, looking for outside-the-box ideas, and finding the spot on the chessboard that is empty and unchartered. It’s also about being transparent, precise and accountable—and delivering on our promises.
3. How do you break through and get clients noticed with digital marketing campaigns?
There are lots of platforms and it’s a noisy world and we aim to cut through the clutter. Everything under the sun has been done, so a big part of it is timing. You have to zig when everyone else is zagging.
4. How do you turn around a bad day?
Honestly? I call my mom. Or I draw or meditate or play tennis. If I get a negative email, I don’t reply right away, I get up and take a break and do one of those things I love.
5. What are you currently most proud of?
I feel like we're on the precipice of doing something really great as a team. So I'm really proud of some of the growth we've had and some of the new people we brought on board. I'm really proud of my partnership with Jeff (Miller, CEO).
6. What's the best piece of advice you ever got?
My dad told me two things before he died. I used to tell him how busy I was, and he’d say, “Son, that list will never be done.” So enjoy life. The second was, “You come into this world naked and you leave it naked. Everything in the middle is borrowed.” So it’s memories and experiences that are important, not things.

CASE STUDY
The client:
Bank of Montreal
The project:
Cost of Borrowing Calculator
Background:
BMO’s online borrowing calculator, used by thousands of employees, was created by another vendor and was outdated and not compliant with BMO’s technology and requirements.
The ask:
Paralucent was asked to rebuild the calculator with more features and improve the user interface using a cloud-first architecture.
The process:
Paralucent provided project management, quality assurance and app development. “This was a particularly challenging project,” said Project Manager and Business Analyst Martha Umar. “The initial scope of the project was ambiguous, which led to several back-and-forths with the BMO team. And the BMO Technology team joined the project asking for the calculator to be developed with angular UI after the first set of calculators (nine) were already developed in .mvc. The project team started working closely with both BMO Business and BMO Technology and included all their asks in the design document.” The project took a team of five almost one year for the first phase, which included a lot of long hours, weekends and at some point, daily deployments to accommodate an ask from Technology.
The result:
The calculator launched in August and is used by the 900+ BMO branches in Canada to process all mortgage renewals, and to help with various processes like loan amendments. “The launch was a huge success and there were no issues after launch, which is rare for a custom application that is this complex,” said Martha. “Feedback from the users has been great.”
